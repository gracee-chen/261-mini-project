\begin{abstract}
We benchmark six image classification methods on Caltech-101 in the context of medical AI, where reliable visual recognition underpins automated diagnosis. Five deep learning architectures (\texttt{ResNet-50}, \texttt{EfficientNet-B2}, \texttt{ViT-B/16}, \texttt{ConvNeXt-Tiny}, and \texttt{EVA-02-Small}) are fine-tuned from pretrained weights under a unified protocol, alongside a classical \texttt{SVM} baseline using frozen \texttt{ResNet-18} features. \texttt{EVA-02-Small} achieves the highest accuracy (96.79\%), with all deep models within a 0.61\,pp range; the \texttt{SVM} reaches 89.50\%. We further conduct ablation studies on input resolution, data augmentation, optimizer choice, and feature representation to isolate the contribution of individual design decisions. Evaluation covers overall and top-5 accuracy, macro/weighted precision, recall, F1-score, per-class accuracy, and confusion matrices. Code and trained models are available at \url{https://github.com/gracee-chen/261-mini-project}.
\end{abstract}
